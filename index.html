Services

SELLER'S PRE-SALE INSPECTION

A full professional home inspection is performed for the property seller, and is conducted before the property is shown. This allows for a full disclosure of the property and proper receipts showing all improvements completed. The seller can set a solid price, eliminating bargaining points, leading to a faster sale.

BUYER'S INSPECTION

As a buyer you want to have a good idea of the condition of the home before you write that check. A buyer's inspection will notify you of any concerns big or small. This information can help in negotiations and prepare you for any upcoming maintenance on your new home.

INSPECTION REPORT WITH PHOTOS

During the inspection our licensed professionals will inspect your home inside and out, top to bottom. This includes details about the condition of the roof (when accessible) as well as the foundation and attic spaces. When the inspection is complete you will receive a detailed report complete with pictures.

Your concerns matter to us! This is why at the end of every inspection our inspectors will speak with you and give you a brief summary of what we found and answer any of your questions. Your written report is ready to review in an easy to understand report within 24 hours of the inspection.

Radon Testing

The radon test takes 48 hours and can be added to any inspection.

It is recommended to get your home tested for Radon Gas every 2 years. We encourage all our home buyers and sellers to get this test done at the time of the inspection.

AIR QUALITY TESTING

Poor air quality can cause several unwanted symptoms such as headache, fatigue, shortness of breath, hypersensitivity and allergies as well as many others. Air quality tests measure the air for things that are known to cause these symptoms. These tests can show whether the quality of your indoor air is either safe or dangerous.

We offer air quality testing so that you can feel safer in your own home. Our inspectors will take samples depending on the size of your home. These samples are then sent to a lab and an official detailed report will be provided.

What is radon gas?

Radon comes from the natural (radioactive) breakdown of uranium in soil, rock, and water, entering the air you breathe. Radon is colorless and odorless. It can infiltrate any type of building -homes, offices, and schools - and build up to high levels. But you and your family will most likely receive your greatest exposure where you spend most of your time in your home. Breathing air that contains radon can cause lung cancer. In fact, the Surgeon General has warned that radon is the second leading cause of lung cancer in the United States today. There are simple, relatively inexpensive measures for radon mitigation that you can take to fix a radon problem and even very high levels can be reduced to acceptable levels. What is inspected?

Exterior Components Landscaping: Its effects on the outside of the house. Siding, Trim, Windows, & Storms: General condition of each. Roof, Chimney, Flashing, & Valleys: Evidence of water penetration, materials and condition. Gutters, Downspouts, & Extensions: General condition, effects of dampness in the basement/crawlspace and its ability to carry off rainwater. Driveways, Walkways, Patios, Decks, & Porches: General condition of each. Air Conditioner Compressor: General condition of its components.

Interior Components Walls, Floors, Ceilings, and Windows: General condition and its effects on the foundation. Plumbing Systems: All visible fixtures, piping and drains testing for operation. Electrical Systems: Outside entrance wiring, service panel and visible wiring inspected plus testing of outlets. Heating System: Visible portion of heating plant and components. Attic: Visible roof sheathing, ventilation, insulation and framing. Basement Crawlspace: Foundation soundness and any evidence of moisture problems.
